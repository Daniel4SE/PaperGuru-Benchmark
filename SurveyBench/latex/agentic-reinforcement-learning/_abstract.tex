Read this section first; everything that follows builds on the definitions given here. \textbf{Executive overview.} Agentic Reinforcement Learning (Agentic RL) is a family of training methods. It treats a large language model (LLM) as a \emph{policy} embedded in an interactive environment. The policy is optimized end-to-end against trajectory-level rewards. This survey defines the field, places it within thirteen years of deep-RL history, and catalogues its taxonomy, algorithms, pipelines, skills, applications, benchmarks, failure modes, and open problems.
