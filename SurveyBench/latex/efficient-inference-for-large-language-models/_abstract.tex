Large language models (LLMs) such as GPT-3 (175B parameters, Brown et al., 2020), LLaMA-2 (7B--70B, Touvron et al., 2023), Mixtral 8x7B, DeepSeek-V2 (236B total / 21B activated, Liu et al., 2024) and DeepSeek-V3 (671B total / 37B activated per token, DeepSeek-AI, 2024) have moved from research curiosities to ubiquitous infrastructure: by early 2025, services such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot and Cursor served billions of LLM tokens per day, with operating cost dominated by \emph{inference} rather than training. Whereas pretraining is a one-off event amortized over a model's lifetime, inference is a continuous workload whose unit economics determine whether an application can scale. This survey concentrates on the methods, systems, and benchmarks that have emerged to drive that unit cost down --- collectively called \emph{efficient LLM inference}.
